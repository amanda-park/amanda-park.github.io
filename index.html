Bio

Amanda Park currently works as a Data Scientist at Spectrum Health. She uses statistical programming languages R and Python to transform data into actionable insights for a large healthcare organization, and has worked on pivotal analytics projects, such as predicting when the surge for COVID-19 patients will hit. She previously obtained her Master's in Statistics from Binghamton University in May 2019 and was the president of the Board of Directors for Youth Power, a nonprofit organization that advocates for youth rights. She believes that everyone's voice deserves to be heard and champions for equality across all realms. Outside of work, she is found either reading a book, dabbling in a nerdy hobby, or trying to muster up the motivation to actually go exercise. She recently has started up a blog, which you can check out in the sidebar above! Some of her major projects are described below.

Pokemon Analysis

It takes a lot of effort to be the very best there ever was, especially with analyzing data. Amanda certainly isn't there yet, but she analyzed a Pokemon data set for a project in grad school and created an interactive R Markdown file to go alongside it.

Mental Health Guidebook

This app was built with Android Studio using Java, and it recently released on the Google Play Store! You can download the app here. The app is an overview of various therapy modalities and exercises one can practice to improve their mental health. For those who can't afford mental health treatment and/or want a supplement to their regular mental health treatment, this app will hopefully offer some support!

Tidy Tuesday

As much as Python has won the popularity contest for programming languages for data science, Amanda still has a soft spot in her heart for the versatility that R packages such as the tidyverse offers in analysis. She has a Github repository including some of her analyses with using R, which include analyzing her Twitter tweets (deployed version found here and scraping web data for Pokemon's Pokedex entries.
